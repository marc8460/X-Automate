Objective
Implement a realistic **Aura Viral Opportunity Score (0–100)** for tweets detected in the X.com feed.
The score should identify tweets that are **good opportunities to reply to early**, based on engagement quality, growth speed, and reply competition.

The system should calculate three normalized scores from 0–100 and take their average.

Final formula:

viralScore = (engagementScore + velocityScore + competitionScore) / 3

The extension will display this as the **Aura Score badge** on tweets.

---

1. Engagement Score (0–100)

Purpose: measure how well the tweet is performing relative to its reach.

First calculate engagement:

engagement = likes + replies + reposts

Then engagement rate:

engagement_rate = engagement / views

Use a smooth curve so that very large tweets do not dominate the scale.

Formula:

engagementScore = min((engagement_rate / 0.10) ^ 0.6, 1) * 100

Interpretation:

0.5% engagement → ~15 score
1% engagement → ~30 score
3% engagement → ~55 score
5% engagement → ~70 score
8% engagement → ~88 score
10%+ engagement → 100 score

---

2. Velocity Score (0–100)

Purpose: detect if the tweet is currently gaining traction.

Calculate time since posting:

minutes = minutes_since_post

Then calculate velocity:

velocity = engagement / minutes

Use a curve so tweets growing faster get significantly higher scores.

Formula:

velocityScore = min((velocity / 3) ^ 0.7, 1) * 100

Interpretation:

0.05 engagement/min → ~10 score
0.2 engagement/min → ~30 score
0.5 engagement/min → ~50 score
1 engagement/min → ~70 score
2 engagement/min → ~85 score
3+ engagement/min → 100 score

---

3. Reply Competition Score (0–100)

Purpose: detect how crowded the replies are.

Calculate reply ratio:

reply_ratio = replies / likes

Lower ratios mean better opportunity to reply.

Formula:

competitionScore = (1 - min(reply_ratio, 1)) * 100

Interpretation:

0.02 → 98 score (very low competition)
0.10 → 90 score
0.25 → 75 score
0.40 → 60 score
0.70 → 30 score
1.0 → 0 score

---

4. Final Aura Viral Score

Compute final score:

viralScore = (engagementScore + velocityScore + competitionScore) / 3

Round to nearest integer.

Example:

likes = 485
replies = 12
reposts = 22
views = 10000
time_since_post = 9h

engagement = 519
engagement_rate = 5.19%
engagementScore ≈ 67

velocity = 519 / 540min = 0.96
velocityScore ≈ 45

reply_ratio = 12 / 485 = 0.024
competitionScore ≈ 98

viralScore = (67 + 45 + 98) / 3 ≈ 70

Aura badge should show:

🔥 70

---

5. Badge Display in Feed

The extension should render the score on each tweet:

🔥 80+ → green (excellent opportunity)
🔥 60–79 → yellow (good opportunity)
🔥 40–59 → gray (average)
🔥 <40 → hidden or muted

Only show scores if:

views > 100 OR likes > 5

to avoid noise from tiny tweets.

---

6. Implementation Notes

The score should be calculated inside the content script when metrics are extracted from the tweet DOM.

Required inputs:

likes
replies
reposts
views
tweet age (minutes)

If views are missing, fall back to engagement-only scoring.

The goal is to prioritize tweets that:

* have strong engagement relative to views
* are growing quickly
* still have low reply competition

These are the best tweets to reply to for visibility.
